Manhattan GPS Watch · working concept

Drop yourself anywhere in Manhattan. Read the cross streets off the watch.

Move the pin on the map — the watch computes which streets you're between using the same grid math that will live in the firmware. No map data, no internet. Try the presets, including one below Houston (negative streets) and one that's off the island.

What each part of the face is doing

  • Big lineThe signature readout — street × avenue, to the nearest half-block. 23.5 × 9 = between 23rd and 24th, by 9th Ave. Below Houston it counts down (0, -1, …). In Alphabet City the avenue shows as its letter: 7.5 × C. Off the island it reads THE SUBURBS.
  • NeighborhoodThe line under the reading names your Manhattan neighborhood, inferred from the cross-street position (fuzzier downtown, where the grid model is weakest).
  • Top-leftDay + date, Casio-style.
  • Top-rightCompass. A single arrow to north, toggled on/off to save power (it's dimmed when off).
  • BottomTime, and when the location was last fixed. In battery-saver mode the reading only updates when you press Refresh.
Grid model fitted to 12 real street corners (OpenStreetMap) plus 8 landmark anchors, spanning the East Village to Harlem. Cross-validated accuracy: streets ~0.3 blocks, avenues ~0.17 blocks through the core of the island. The off-island boundary follows the real coastline (point-in-polygon), so it's synced to the shoreline. Far downtown (below ~14th St) is extrapolated and can be ~1 block off; the West Village in particular genuinely isn't a square grid (it predates the 1811 plan), so readings there are approximate. A design + algorithm prototype, not firmware.
