manhattan gps watch

A watch project. The idea: a Casio F-91W, but with GPS, that tells you which Manhattan cross streets you're standing between instead of just the time. Stand at 72nd and 5th and it reads 72 × 5.

This is the software half, proven before any soldering. A small grid model turns a raw GPS coordinate into a Manhattan cross street, names your neighborhood, switches Alphabet City over to letters, counts into the negatives below Houston, and reads THE SUBURBS the moment you leave the island. No map data, no internet, no server.

→ interactive map (drag a pin anywhere in Manhattan) → live GPS (open on your phone in NYC and walk around)
Next step: get it running on a real chip.
